User Research · May 2026
This is not just an idea.
It has been validated.

Before a single screen was designed, real Ajo users were interviewed to understand what actually matters — and what would make or break adoption of a digital version. The findings shaped every design decision behind MonieCircle.

The problem is real. The appetite is conditional. And Moniepoint is uniquely positioned to meet those conditions.

2
Real Ajo users interviewed — both with multiple completed cycles
5
Core insight themes identified across both interviews
100%
Of respondents confirmed coordination, reminders, and payout delays are genuine pain points worth solving
Finding 01
Trust is everything — and it is personal
Both respondents built groups exclusively through personal networks. Neither would join a group with strangers regardless of app verification. MonieCircle is private-group only — by design, not accident.

"You are the one bringing them into the group. You are recommending them. And if they misbehave, it's going to be on you."

— Respondent 1, Active Ajo Coordinator
Finding 02
The coordinator bears every burden
Reminders, deadlines, disputes, covering gaps — all fall on one person. MonieCircle automates the coordinator's most painful tasks: reminders, contribution tracking, and payout disbursement.

"Bringing people together — that is the most stressful part. And you have to remind them, this is the due date, enforcing the boundaries."

— Respondent 1
Finding 03
Delayed payouts are a real, recurring pain
Respondent 2 experienced delayed payouts on two occasions — coordinator personally bridging shortfalls while waiting on late contributors. MonieCircle's auto-deduction and Monie Sense default prediction address this directly. Moniepoint's agent network in all 36 states removes "I couldn't transfer" as an excuse.
Finding 04
The app must feel like an organiser, not a bank
Both respondents were uncomfortable with the app holding money. They want facilitation — not a financial custodian. MonieCircle is positioned as "your Ajo, organised" — not a savings product.

"The app verifying them does not automatically mean that they cannot withdraw and decide to discontinue."

— Respondent 2
Finding 05
Fee model must be opt-in, not imposed
Respondent 1 rejected a per-cycle fee outright. Respondent 2 accepted it only if the group collectively agrees before the cycle begins. MonieCircle's fee is transparent and group-agreed at setup — never silently deducted.
Finding 06
A safety net would meaningfully increase trust
Respondent 2 said a traceability mechanism or payout guarantee would significantly increase confidence. This validates Monie Sense's Trust Score — a behavioural reliability rating shown before anyone joins a circle.
Validation Verdict
The problem is real.
The appetite is conditional.
Both respondents confirmed genuine friction in traditional Ajo. Adoption depends on the app feeling like a trusted tool for known groups. MonieCircle was designed precisely around these conditions.
✅ Private groups only — closed circles of people who already know each other
✅ App as organiser — not a bank or financial custodian
✅ Coordinator-first design — automates their most stressful tasks
✅ Transparent fee model — group agrees upfront, no platform surprises
✅ Trust Score safety net — behavioural reliability before joining any circle

01 · HeroMonieCircle"Ajo, organised"
Your community, your turn+

What it is

A fully transparent, in-app rotating savings group built for private circles of trusted people. Admin creates the group, adds members by phone or account number, sets amount, payout rotation, and schedule. Every member sees the live balance. The admin cannot hide a kobo.

Research-backed: Both respondents confirmed they only join groups with people they personally know. Private-group only — by design.

Why it wins

  • Automates the coordinator's most stressful tasks
  • Full live transparency eliminates admin fraud
  • Auto-deduction removes the monthly chasing
  • 36-state agent network enables cash-in anywhere
  • Group-agreed fee model — not silently imposed
  • Legal disclaimer protects Moniepoint's position

Potential impact

Brings millions of informal Ajo groups onto a regulated, transparent platform — reducing fraud, protecting coordinators, and creating a viral network effect as members recruit each other into Moniepoint's ecosystem.

First major bank to own Ajo at scale
02MonieGoal TogetherMutual Savings
Same dream, shared hustle+

What it is

Two or more people set a shared savings goal — a car, rent, a trip, a business. Each contributes to one pot, and all parties see the real-time breakdown of who contributed what and how close they are to the goal.

Why it wins

  • No direct equivalent in Nigerian fintech
  • Shared accountability drives higher completion rates
  • Transparency removes trust issues between partners
  • Interest can accrue on pooled savings

Potential impact

Captures couples, friends, and business partners who currently manage shared goals in spreadsheets and WhatsApp threads.

No direct competitor in Nigeria
03MonieVaultMutual Control
Shared money, shared trust+

What it is

A shared wallet where any withdrawal requires approval from all parties. Interest-bearing, multi-party, with built-in approval gates before any money moves. For couples, co-founders, and family savings pools.

Why it wins

  • Approval flow prevents impulsive withdrawals
  • Interest on locked savings rewards discipline
  • Perfect for couples, co-founders, family savings
  • Completely unique in Nigerian fintech

Potential impact

A trust layer for shared finances — saving together without fighting about spending — with zero competition in Nigeria today.

Completely uncharted territory
04MoniePocketParent to Child
Send love, schedule it+

What it is

A parent-controlled wallet linked to a child's Moniepoint account. Parents fund it, set automatic allowance schedules, and approve or decline withdrawal requests. The child must give a reason before any money moves. Parents get a weekly AI spend digest.

Why it wins

  • Solves the busy parent problem entirely
  • Scheduled allowances remove guilt and missed payments
  • Teaches children real financial responsibility
  • Nigeria's Greenlight — built for local reality

Potential impact

Captures students, boarders, and their parents — an entirely new user segment. Zero Nigerian fintech has this. Every busy Nigerian parent will want it immediately.

Nigeria's first family finance feature
MonieCircle · User Flow
How MonieCircle works,
step by step.

Transparent, fraud-proof, coordinator-first. Built entirely within the Moniepoint personal app. No new app. No extra signup. Every decision maps to a validated user insight.

Step 01
Create Circle
Admin creates a private group, sets name, contribution amount, start date, and payout rotation. Members added by phone or account number only.
Research: Groups built from personal networks only.
Step 02
Members Accept
Each member reviews full terms — amount, payout position, schedule — before confirming. Nobody joins blind or uninformed.
Step 03 · Key
Full Transparency
Every member sees a live dashboard — who paid, who has not, circle balance, next payout. Admin cannot hide anything from anyone.
Research: Trust depends on visibility.
Step 04 · Key
Auto-Deduction
Moniepoint auto-debits on due date. Monie Sense nudges low-balance members 3 days early. Cash-in via any Moniepoint agent nationwide.
Research: Delayed payouts are the number one coordinator pain.
Step 05
Payout Day
Funds minus group-agreed fee auto-transfer to that month's recipient on schedule. Zero manual action. Zero coordinator stress.
Step 06
Cycle Repeats
Circle auto-resets for the next recipient. Full history permanently logged. Circle renews or dissolves after the final payout.
The AI Layer
Monie Sense —
the intelligence layer Nigerian fintech does not have yet.

Revolut just launched AIR. Starling has Gemini. Nubank has predictive intelligence. Monie Sense brings all of that — built for Nigeria's languages, behaviours, and realities.

For MonieCircle · Research-backed
Default Prediction
AI monitors each member's income and spending. If someone's balance drops close to their due date, Monie Sense nudges them privately — before the group notices, before it becomes drama. Directly addresses the number one coordinator pain point from our interviews.
For MonieCircle · Research-backed
Ajo Trust Score
Behavioural reliability rating based on Moniepoint transaction history — income consistency, savings behaviour, bill payments. Shown before joining any circle. Directly answers Respondent 2's call for a traceability mechanism. No credit bureau needed.
For MonieGoal Together
Goal Pace Intelligence
AI tracks group contribution pace and tells members: "At this rate, you will hit your goal 3 weeks late — here is how to close the gap." Accountability without the awkward conversation.
For MoniePocket
Parent Intelligence Digest
AI learns the child's spending patterns weekly. Parents get a digest: "Your child tends to run low mid-week. Consider scheduling 5,000 naira on Wednesdays." Parenting, powered by data.
Across All Features
Conversational Money Assistant
Voice-note or type in Pidgin, Yoruba, Igbo, or English: "How far, how much I don spend on food this month?" Monie Sense answers instantly. Nigeria's first truly local AI money assistant.
Across All Features
Financial Health Score
Weekly personalised score based on income, spending, and savings patterns. Not generic advice — your data, your score, your specific next step. Built like Nubank's intelligence, spoken like a Nigerian.
